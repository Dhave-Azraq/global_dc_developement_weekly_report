The global data center sector continues its unprecedented infrastructure supercycle. Colliers reports that 2025 DC investment topped $580 billion, surpassing oil investment for the first time — a structural shift marking data centers as one of the world's most capital-intensive infrastructure categories. Meta dominates headlines this week, boosting its El Paso investment sixfold to $10 billion and announcing plans to fund 10 gas-fired power plants totaling 7.5 GW for its Hyperion campus in Louisiana. Google secured approval for a multibillion-dollar campus in West Virginia. Meanwhile, the regulatory backlash intensifies with four moratoriums passed in a single week across Baltimore, Boone NC, Washington County TN, and Tulsa OK. Senators Hawley and Warren are pushing mandatory energy reporting for data centers. ConstructConnect continues to track 65 projects worth $92.1 billion with potential start dates within six months.
