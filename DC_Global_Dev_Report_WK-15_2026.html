The global data center sector's infrastructure supercycle accelerated further this week. Moody's Ratings confirmed that the six largest US hyperscalers are on track to spend approximately $700 billion in capex in 2026 — nearly six times 2022 levels — warning that the surge is straining free cash flow and increasing debt reliance. Crusoe announced a 900 MW AI factory campus in Abilene, TX for Microsoft, bringing the site's total capacity to 2.1 GW and cementing West Texas as America's "Silicon Prairie." In Missouri, Festus City Council approved CRG/Clayco's $6B hyperscale data center despite hundreds of residents packing a high school gym in opposition — the latest flashpoint in the deepening community backlash nationwide. Denver advanced a yearlong DC moratorium amid drought, Maine moved to become the first state to freeze large DC construction, and the Eastern Band of Cherokee Indians proposed an indefinite ban on tribal lands. ConstructConnect's April report showed 2026 YTD construction starts at $36.9B, tracking to $116B full-year. Amazon raised its Spain investment to €33.7B ($39.5B), its largest European commitment. Meanwhile, Poolside's 2 GW CoreWeave deal collapsed, signaling credit stress in the neocloud segment.
